Interview thesis brief

The case I would bring into the room.

Prepared from the job posting, current public signals, and verified career evidence. Company-specific recommendations are hypotheses for discovery unless directly sourced.

Actual mandate

Convert GenAI experimentation into compounding enterprise capability. Build the proving discipline between enterprise demand and scaled AI: better problem framing, explicit authority and risk boundaries, enterprise-representative POCs, realistic stress testing, clear scale/partner/stop decisions, and deliberate reuse of capability and evidence.

Company moment

  • KPMG has publicly expanded major AI relationships and platforms across cloud ecosystems.
  • Leadership has described a move from experimentation and POCs toward implementation and scaling.
  • Recent KPMG-reported research highlights weak visibility into AI operating costs and the importance of AI economics.
  • The job itself spans GenAI development, POCs, cross-functional coordination, partners, thought leadership, roadmaps, and business impact.

Core tensions

  • Experiment velocity ↔ enterprise assurance
  • POC novelty ↔ reuse and economics
  • Platform optionality ↔ common architecture and controls
  • Technical depth ↔ stakeholder translation
  • Central innovation ↔ domain professional judgment

Candidate thesis

Innovation compounds when every experiment leaves behind one of three assets: a reusable capability, reusable evidence, or a fast no. The GenAI Proving Ground is the operating concept: FRAME the work, BOUND authority and risk, BUILD the minimum enterprise proof, STRESS quality/economics/adoption, then COMPOUND through scale, reuse, partnership, or disciplined retirement.

Decision architecture

  • What changes in the workflow?
  • Where does human authority remain?
  • Build, buy, partner, or reuse?
  • What evidence warrants scale?
  • What gets standardized?
  • What gets stopped?

Balanced scorecard

  • Business movement
  • Adoption behavior
  • AI task performance and failure modes
  • Trust, controls, and auditability
  • Run cost and reuse economics
  • Time from idea to decision-quality evidence

Proof map

Vape-Jet: direct AI-first operating transformation across factory, customer, support, engineering issue flow, ERP, collaboration systems, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows. DudeWorth: direct AI-readiness and opportunity frameworks plus agentic workflow patterns. Amazon: scaled 24/7 launch and operating mechanisms. Compunetics: high-reliability technical program, engineering, manufacturing, quality, and control systems. ZeusVu: regulated autonomy, computer-vision concepts, chain of custody, and a 100% safety record with no FAA incidents.

Strongest objection

The role asks for recent AI/ML depth and hands-on enterprise AI platform development. The verified evidence supports AI workflow and agentic design, Python/TensorFlow exposure, AI-first transformation, technical leadership, regulated autonomy, and robotics/machine-vision operations—but not a career foundation-model research identity or unverified Azure AI Foundry / Vertex AI production ownership.

Response strategy

Own the gap. Make the transferability case around the role's broader stated mandate: POCs, cross-functional engineering, roadmaps, partners, resource priorities, and business impact. Demonstrate technical judgment through the decision lab and discuss platform-specific depth candidly in interview.

90-day posture

0–30: map representative experiments, stakeholders, decision rights, and failure points. 31–60: test the experiment contract on two or three different opportunity types and observe what evidence stakeholders actually need. 61–90: publish reusable evaluation patterns, clarify scale/partner/stop cadence, harvest reusable capabilities, and make operating handoff explicit.

Executive questions

  1. Which GenAI experiments are stuck, and what is usually missing?
  2. Who has final scale/stop authority, and what evidence do they trust?
  3. What must be common across platform ecosystems, and what should remain optional?
  4. Which workflows justify custom AI capability?
  5. How is meaningful adoption distinguished from usage?
  6. Which AI failure mode would most damage trust?
  7. What learning from POCs is reusable today?
  8. Who owns AI unit economics after scale?
